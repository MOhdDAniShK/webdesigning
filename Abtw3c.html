What is W3C

The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) develops web standards ensuring interoperability, accessibility, and consistent behavior across web technologies and browsers.
The W3C, founded in 1994 by Tim Berners-Lee, creates guidelines for HTML, CSS, XML, and other web protocols. It promotes open standards, international collaboration, and long-term growth of the web, enabling developers to build compatible, reliable, and accessible websites and applications worldwide.
